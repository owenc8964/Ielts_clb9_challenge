## Day 16 — Task 2
**題目：** Should governments provide free housing to marginalized groups?
**CLB：** 6.0（TR 6.5 / CC 6.0 / LR 6.0 / GRA 5.5）

As housing prices continue to surge globally, the question of whether the state should provide free housing to marginalized groups has become increasingly significant. While some argue against such heavy public expenditure, I strongly believe that ensuring basic shelter is a fundamental government responsibility. Ultimately, this approach is not merely a welfare policy — it is a commitment to social stability.

A primary reason for supporting state-funded housing is that satisfying basic human needs is crucial for maintaining social order. When a growing portion of the population is forced into homelessness, it often exacerbates urban challenges, directly feeding into higher crime rates such as theft and robbery. By treating housing as a basic right, the government takes proactive intervention rather than spending more budget later to tackle the consequences of a compromised judicial and public health system.

Furthermore, the current real estate market presents structural challenges that individual efforts alone can hardly overcome. In many regions, particularly in Taiwan, housing has become prohibitively expensive. The disproportionate ratio of stagnant wages to soaring property indexes means that even hard-working young adults cannot afford a home. If left unaddressed, this severe economic pressure can make the youth feel apathetic towards their careers, leading to the "lying flat" phenomenon where people simply drop out of the workforce. Therefore, the government must allocate resources appropriately to correct this market failure.

In conclusion, I firmly agree that the government should provide housing assistance to those in need. The real challenge lies not in whether to offer this welfare, but in how policymakers can effectively distribute resources to reduce inequality. Empowering citizens with a stable living environment will eventually foster a healthier, more cohesive society.

## Day 17 — Task 2
**題目：** What are the causes and effects of the throwaway culture?
**CLB：** 6.5（TR 7.0 / CC 6.5 / LR 6.5 / GRA 6.0）

The throwaway culture has become increasingly prevalent in this generation, characterized by the convenience of purchasing new products and rapidly discarding old ones. Consequently, this phenomenon exacerbates resource depletion and leads to severe environmental degradation.

This culture primarily stems from both corporate strategies and consumer demand. On the supply side, businesses often employ planned obsolescence to ensure continuous sales. To accommodate the public's desire for affordability and variety, fast-fashion brands like Zara mass-produce low-cost garments. While this appears to be a win-win situation for profit margins and consumer satisfaction, it fundamentally undermines intergenerational equity.

The environmental impact of this linear consumption model is devastating. The entire lifecycle of these products — from manufacturing to transportation and ultimate disposal — generates massive carbon emissions and pollution. If the public continues to blindly succumb to this throwaway mentality, we will only exacerbate the ecological crisis, making it incredibly difficult to resolve.

In conclusion, resolving this issue requires a profound sense of civic responsibility. It is not merely about resisting a materialistic culture; rather, both corporations and individuals must prioritize environmental sustainability over short-term convenience. Furthermore, governments must intervene with stringent policies to curb resource depletion and enforce proper end-of-life management for consumer goods.

## Day 18 — Task 2
**題目：** Should online education replace traditional classrooms?
**CLB：** 7.5（TR 8.0 / CC 7.5 / LR 8.0 / GRA 6.5）🔥

As technology advances, education is evolving not only in its methods but also in its mediums. While online education has progressed significantly, leading some to believe it should completely replace traditional schooling, I wholeheartedly support prioritizing digital learning while retaining conventional classrooms due to their irreplaceable functions.

Traditional classrooms typically offer an environment suited for passive knowledge transmission. In contrast, online education allows students to tailor their learning pace through the democratization of information. For instance, computers enable students to effortlessly record, search, and review supplementary references, thereby maximizing their learning potential.

However, traditional classrooms play a crucial role in a child's holistic development, as they cultivate social cohesion and spontaneous brainstorming. Through collaborative projects, students learn to allocate tasks and work as a unified force — a critical interpersonal function that cannot be easily replicated by virtual platforms.

Therefore, we should not render traditional schools obsolete; rather, educators must exercise great wisdom to strike a perfect balance, thereby achieving the best of both worlds.

## Day 19 — Task 2
**題目：** AI and automation are increasingly used in various professions. Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?
**CLB：** 7.0（TR 7.5 / CC 7.0 / LR 7.0 / GRA 6.0）

As artificial intelligence and automation become increasingly prevalent in this generation, they are being widely adopted across various professions. This transformative trend has fundamentally reshaped the world. In my opinion, the advantages of integrating AI significantly outweigh the disadvantages.

First of all, AI can lay the foundational groundwork and enhance diagnostic accuracy, thereby freeing professionals from mundane administrative tasks. This effectively mitigates employee burnout and boosts their overall job satisfaction. Therefore, the human workforce can be reallocated to applications with greater potential.

Nevertheless, an overreliance on these systems may lead to data breaches of confidential information, or bring operations to a complete standstill during ransomware attacks. As AI systems play an increasingly central role, such incidents may occur more frequently. Furthermore, companies may find their data locked or rendered inaccessible if they violate the terms of service set by AI providers, which can result in substantial financial losses.

Given the gigantic potential of AI and automation, I firmly believe their benefits far outweigh the drawbacks. Nonetheless, organizations utilizing these technologies must prioritize cybersecurity and adhere to usage restrictions; otherwise, it may lead to severe consequences.

## Day 20 — Task 2
**題目：** Some people think that private cars should be completely banned in city centers. Do you agree or disagree?
**CLB：** 7.0（TR 7.5 / CC 7.0 / LR 7.5 / GRA 6.5）🔥

The use of private cars causes severe traffic and environmental problems in many cities. Some people even argue that it is necessary to completely ban private cars in city centers. Therefore, this topic sparks widespread debate. Nevertheless, I do not believe this drastic strategy can truly resolve the problem.

First of all, imposing a blanket ban is an impractical approach, as inadequate public transit infrastructure would severely restrict personal mobility. Such a policy would dictate people's schedules and routes, preventing them from easily reaching their destinations, which would inevitably lead to intense public dissatisfaction. In Taiwan, for instance, most cities lack an MRT system, and citizens prefer driving over taking unreliable buses. If an extreme policy restricts vehicles under these circumstances, it will cause severe disruptions to daily life.

The core issue lies in managing road usage efficiently, meaning policymakers should implement targeted interventions rather than broad prohibitions. Instead of banning private cars, governments should deploy additional commuter shuttles during peak hours to alleviate traffic gridlock. In Neihu, for example, countless employees endure severe congestion during rush hours; introducing dedicated shuttles successfully relieves this bottleneck while still accommodating necessary private vehicle usage.

In conclusion, comprehensive measures are far more effective than extreme restrictions, and they do not cause severe disruptions to daily life. That is why I disagree with a complete ban. Governments must shoulder their responsibilities and meticulously analyze traffic patterns to formulate sustainable solutions for all road users.
